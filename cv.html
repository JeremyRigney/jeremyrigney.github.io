Experience

Trinity College Dublin (June - August 2019)
Intern
During this internship at the Irish LOFAR station with Trinity College Dublin, I was involved in a number of elements of the newly launched education and outreach programme. This involved working as part of a team of three; researching, developing content, promotion and delivery of 8 workshops to young people between the ages of 5 and 13. I led regular guided tours of the telescopes, communicating past telescope history and current progress to groups of up to 50 people as well as assisting with public outreach events including Astronomy on Tap and the IAU 100 exhibition. In addition I had an opportunity to observe using I-LOFAR to test pulsar period detecting software. This internship allowed me to build science communication skills as well as affording the opportunity to develop knowledge of I-LOFAR software.

University College Dublin (June - August 2018)
Intern
Completed summer internship in the UCD Physics High Energy Astrophysics Research Group, examining the feasibility of Blazar monitoring in the low frequency regime. I had the opportunity to observe with I-LOFAR, the Irish Low Frequency Array, a new radio telescope built on the grounds of Birr Castle in 2017. I created a poster on the research I conducted which was presented to UCD Physics faculty in the final week, as part of the internship. Upon completion of the internship I presented my poster at the 2018 Irish National Astronomy Meeting. I developed research and data analysis skills during my time as an intern. Furthermore I developed my programming skills in learning to control and observe with I-LOFAR.
